Gyumri, Armenia

Gyumri

Gyumri is the second largest city in Armenia, the administrative center of the Shirak region. The city is located 126 kilometers from Yerevan in the central part of the Shirak basin, surrounded from the north, east and southeast by the Shirak and Pambak ranges and spurs of the Aragats mountain range.

Attractions of Gyumri

Black Fortress

The Black Fortress was built in the middle of the 19th century after the end of the Russian-Turkish war of 1828-1829.

Vardanants Square

the largest central square in Gyumri. Named in memory of the battle of the Armenian army led by Vardan Mamikonyan with the Persian army on the Avarayr field.

Gyumri's Mother Armenia

It was erected in 1975 on a hill to the west of the city of Gyumri. It was created by sculptors Ara Sargsyan, Gaspar Gasparyan and Yerem Vartanyan.

Mher Mkrtchyan Museum

The most famous actor that came from Gyumri was Mher Mkrtchyan. His fame extended beyond Armenia and was also celebrated across the USSR. His house-turned-museum is truly an amazing place.

Amenaprkich Church

During the existence of the church, it has endured two large earthquakes and survived the first one that happened in 1926.

Saint Nikolai the Wonderworker Russian Church

This charming church was built in the 19th-century in Gyumri. Church showed a great example of Gothic and mysterious architecture in Gyumri.

Armenia's Oldest Barbershop

For over 80 years, this barbershop has operated almost like a men's club and stands as Armenia's oldest barbershop..

Poloz Mukuch Restaurant

Florence is a place where old meets new in a two-story, traditional building that has been given a colorful facelift inside. It exudes a cozy atmosphere where you can dine with family or friends in a fun and lively setting.

Kumayri Historic District

Mesmerizing with its architecture, the first settlements in Kumayri date back to the 5th century BC.

Independence Square

The second biggest square in Gyumri is Independence Square. Built during WW2, this large square is surrounded by green yards and a park.

Museum of the Aslamazyan Sisters

Born at the turn of the 20th-century, Mariam and Eranuhi Aslamazyan traveled around the world and documented their experiences through paintings, sculptures, and ceramics.
